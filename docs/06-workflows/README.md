# Chapter 06 — Workflows

## Purpose

This chapter defines the engineering workflows of Decidera.

While the Architecture describes the structural organization of the system, Workflows describe how engineering activities progress from initiation to completion.

Workflows coordinate the movement of engineering knowledge while preserving traceability, consistency, and reproducibility.

---

## Scope

This chapter describes:

- Engineering workflows
- Workflow responsibilities
- Workflow lifecycle
- Workflow coordination
- Workflow boundaries
- Workflow composition

Implementation details are intentionally excluded.

---

## Relationship with Previous Chapters

The Domain Model defines the engineering vocabulary.

The Architecture defines the structural organization.

The Workflows define how the architecture is used to perform engineering work.

Together they establish the operational behavior of Decidera.

---

## Workflow Philosophy

Every workflow should:

- Have a clear purpose.
- Produce a well-defined outcome.
- Preserve engineering traceability.
- Be deterministic whenever possible.
- Be reusable.
- Remain independent of specific implementations.

Workflows should represent engineering processes rather than technical procedures.

---

## Workflow Characteristics

Engineering workflows are designed to be:

- Modular
- Composable
- Traceable
- Repository-centric
- Observable
- Extensible

Each workflow contributes to the Decision-to-Artifact lifecycle without violating the Design Foundation.

## Summary

Workflows describe how Decidera transforms engineering intent into engineering outcomes.

They provide the operational foundation that connects the architectural structure with future implementations while preserving consistency, modularity, and traceability.
